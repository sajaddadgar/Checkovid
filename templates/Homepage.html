Checkovid is a covid-19 fact-checking website that can predict the credit of a claim, news, and tweets about covid-19. We used natural language processing and different machine learning and deep learning algorithms to predict fake news. For predicting these claims, we implement 3 approaches: paragraph-based, sentence-based, and network-based. Also, you can see the similarity of your input claim with other claims in our dataset with cosine similarty.

Paragraph-based

  • Checkovid - Covid19 Fake Claims

We get the list of the fact-checking organization provided by the Reporters' Lab at Duke University. We collected all of the tweet's URLs about coronavirus and covid19 and classified them into REAL and FAKE news based on these fact-checking websites. Finally, we extract tweets' IDs and get all the information about the tweets with Twitter API. This dataset contains 18,000 tweets and all network information about users and tweets divided into REAL and FAKE tweets.

Access the dataset

Sentence-based

  • Checkovid - Covid19 Fake Sentences

For spotting fake news among the real news, We divided each paragraph in the dataset into sentences using the Spacy library and remove pointless sentences Manually. This dataset contains 15,636 sentences that are classified based on its paragraph verdict.

Access the dataset
